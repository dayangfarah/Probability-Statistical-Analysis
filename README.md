* **Academic Session/Semester:** 20232024/2 
* **Credit Hours:** 3 

---

## 📖 Course Synopsis
This course is designed to introduce some statistical techniques as tools to analyse the data. In the beginning the students will be exposed with various forms of data. The data represented by the different types of variables are derived from different sources; daily and industrial activities. 

The analysis begins with the data representation visually. The course will also explore some methods of parameter estimation from different distributions. Further data analysis is conducted by introducing the hypothesis testing. Some models are employed to fit groups of data. At the end of course the students should be able to apply some statistical models in analysing data using available software.

## 💭 Reflection

### What I Learned
Through SECI 1143, I bridged the gap between raw, unstructured numbers and meaningful insights. Moving beyond theoretical probability formulas, I gained a structured understanding of how to clean, visually represent, and interpret data patterns. Mastering hypothesis testing was a key milestone, as it shifted my mindset from merely "looking" at data to scientifically validating claims and making data-driven decisions.

### Challenges & Growth
Shifting from purely manual statistical calculations to scripting with **R Programming** for data analysis was highly rewarding. Translating statistical logic into automated code-such as generating box plots or executing ANOVA tests-felt challenging initially but proved to be an invaluable skill for data science. Collaborating on the group projects also refined my ability to collect messy real-world data and synthesize it into a clean, professional technical report.

### Future Application
The competencies built in this course—particularly data description, inference testing, and R-based visualization—serve as the direct bedrock for my journey into Data Science and Artificial Intelligence. I plan to leverage these statistical foundations to build cleaner data pipelines, understand machine learning models at a mathematical level, and back up my future project engineering decisions with rigorous empirical proof.
